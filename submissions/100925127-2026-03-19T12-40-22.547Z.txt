SECTION C — COMPULSORY:
1(a). Five Proofs that Jesus is God

1. Virgin Birth (Supernatural Conception)
Explanation: The seed for Jesus' conception was provided by the Holy Spirit, not from a human father. This shows He is divine and did not inherit sin from the male seed (Adam)
Scripture Reference: Matthew 1:18–23

2. Sinlessness
Explanation: Jesus never sinned. He always obeyed God perfectly, showing His divine nature.
Scripture References: 2 Corinthians 5:21; 1 Peter 2:22; 1 John 3:5

3. Forgiving Sins
Explanation: Jesus forgave sins, something only God can do. This proves His authority and deity.
Scripture Reference: Matthew 9:2

4. Called “God” by the Father
Explanation: God the Father directly addressed Jesus as God, confirming His divine status.
Scripture Reference: Hebrews 1:8

5. Visible Expression of the Invisible God
Explanation: Jesus shows us what God is like. Seeing Him is seeing the invisible God in a visible way. In other words, when we see Jesus, we have seen God.
Scripture Reference: Hebrews 1:3

1(b). Why the virgin birth was necessary theologically

Explanation: Sin is passed down through the male seed (Adam) making it a genetic transmission of sin. If Jesus had a human father, He would have inherited this sinful nature. So the Holy Spirit caused Jesus to be conceived in Mary without a human father, hence Jesus was sinless from birth.
Being sinless was necessary for Jesus to be the perfect Savior who could take away the sins of the world. This shows that His birth was divine and supernatural, not ordinary.

Question 2:
The hypostatic union of Christ means that Jesus Christ has two complete natures in Himself. He is 100% God and 100% man at the same time.
His divine nature gives Him all the power, knowledge, and holiness of God.
His human nature allows Him to fully experience what it is like to be human, including suffering, temptation, and death.
Because He is both God and man, Jesus can connect humans to God perfectly. He understands our struggles as a human yet has the authority and power of God.
This union allows Him to be the perfect mediator who can represent us before God and bring God’s blessings to us.

1. Jesus got tired
Explanation: Jesus experienced fatigue and tiredness like humans, showing He had a real human body.
Scripture Reference: John 4:6

2. Jesus felt hunger and thirst.
Explanation: He needed food and drink, showing He experienced human physical needs.
Scripture Reference: Matthew 4:2; John 19:28

3. Jesus experienced emotions
Explanation: He felt sadness, grief, and compassion, just like humans do.
Scripture References: John 11:35 and Matthew 26:37–38

4. Jesus experienced pain and suffering.
Explanation: He felt real physical pain, especially on the cross, showing His full humanity.
Scripture Reference: Luke 22:44 and Matthew 27:26

5. Jesus Died
Explanation: He truly died, proving He shared the human experience of mortality.
Scripture Reference: Luke 23:46

Question 6:
Crura fracture was a Roman procedure used to break the legs of those crucified. This was done to hasten death because breaking the legs prevented the person from supporting themselves to breathe.

Exemption in Jesus’ Case:
The soldiers did not break Jesus’ legs during His crucifixion. Instead, He died naturally from His wounds.
This exemption fulfilled prophecy from the Old Testament, showing He was the Messiah.

Relevant Old Testament prophecy:
1. Psalm 34:20: “He protects all his bones; not one of them will be broken.”
This verse predicted that the Messiah’s bones would remain unbroken, even during death.

Fulfillment in the Gospels:
John 19:33 states that when the soldiers came to break the legs of those crucified, Jesus was already dead, so His legs were not broken, fulfilling the prophecy exactly.

This act demonstrates that Jesus’ death was divinely planned and confirms His identity as the Messiah and the perfect Lamb of God.
By exempting His legs from breaking, God confirmed Jesus’ identity as the sinless Savior.
It also shows that every detail of His death was purposeful, connecting the Old Testament promises to the New Testament fulfillment.
