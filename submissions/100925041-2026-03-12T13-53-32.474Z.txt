SECTION C — COMPULSORY:
1(a). 
1. His Supernatural Birth (The Virgin Conception)
The teaching emphasizes that Jesus’ birth was not the result of a human union but a divine act. Because sin is transmitted through the "male seed" (the Adamic nature), a natural birth would have produced a sinful man. By being conceived by the Holy Spirit, Jesus bypassed the sin nature, proving His origin is from God.
Scripture Reference:Luke 1:35 The Holy Spirit will come on you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; so the holy one to be born will be called the Son of God."

2. Divine Titles and Names
Jesus is given titles that are reserved exclusively for Deity. Specifically, the prophecy in Isaiah identifies the coming child not just as a messenger, but as the "Mighty God" and "Everlasting Father." The name Emmanuel also serves as a legal and spiritual declaration that in Jesus, God is physically present with humanity.
Scripture Reference:Isaiah 9:6 "For unto us a child is born... and his name shall be called... The mighty God, The everlasting Father..."

3. The Direct Testimony of God the Father
The teaching points out that the highest authority in the universe—God the Father—formally addressed Jesus as God. In the book of Hebrews, the Father distinguishes Jesus from angels by addressing Him as "God" and affirming His eternal throne.
Scripture Reference:Hebrews 1:8 — "But unto the Son he saith, Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever: a scepter of righteousness is the scepter of thy kingdom."

4. His Power to Forgive Sins
The teaching argues that while a man can forgive a wrong done against himself, only God can forgive the guilt of sin against the divine law. In the Gospels, Jesus forgave sins directly. He then performed physical miracles (like healing the paralytic) specifically to prove that He possessed the divine authority to forgive.
Scripture Reference:Matthew 9:6"But that ye may know that the Son of man hath power on earth to forgive sins, (then saith he to the sick of the palsy,) Arise, take up thy bed..."

5. His Acceptance of Worship
In the Bible, angels and apostles strictly forbade people from worshipping them (stating they were fellow servants). However, Jesus consistently accepted worship from His disciples, the healed, and the blind. By accepting this worship without correction, He affirmed His status as God.
Scripture Reference:Matthew 14:33"Then they that were in the ship came and worshipped him, saying, Of a truth thou art the Son of God."(Also John 20:28, where Thomas calls Him "My Lord and my God").

1(b). 

1. The Federal Headship and the "Male Seed"
The teaching asserts that the "sin nature" is transmitted genetically through the male line. Since Adam was the federal head of the human race and the one who first sinned, the "infection" of sin is carried in the male seed. If Jesus had been conceived through the seed of a natural father (like Joseph), He would have automatically inherited the Adamic sin nature.

2. Bypassing the Adamic Nature
For Jesus to be a qualified Savior, He had to be a perfect, sinless sacrifice. The teaching explains that by bypassing a human father, the "chain" of inherited sin was broken. The Holy Spirit "supplied the seed" or overshadowed Mary, ensuring that while Jesus was fully human (from Mary), He was born without the corruption of Adam’s sin.

3. The Divine Requirement for a Sinless Mediator
Theologically, a sinner cannot pay for the sins of another sinner. If Jesus had been born with inherited sin, His death would have only been for His own transgressions. The virgin birth ensured He was the "Lamb without blemish," possessing a divine nature that was completely holy from conception.

Luke 1:35:"The Holy Spirit will come on you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; so the holy one to be born will be called the Son of God."* (The teaching emphasizes the word *"Holy"* here to show He was born without the stain of sin).

Question 2:
The "Hypostatic Union" is the theological term used to describe the union of two distinct natures—100% Divine and 100% Human—in the one Person of Jesus Christ. The teaching explains that these two natures are not "mixed" to create a third type of being, nor are they "alternated." Instead, they exist simultaneously and perfectly in Him.

*The Perfect Mediator:* To be a mediator, one must relate perfectly to both parties in a dispute. 
As 100% Man, Jesus could represent humanity, feel our infirmities, and legally die in our place to pay the penalty for sin.
As 100% God, His sacrifice had infinite value, and He had the power to conquer death and satisfy the divine justice of God. 
Conclusively, Only a Being who is both God and Man can lay a hand on both and reconcile them (Job 9:33).

Crura fracture, historically known as crurifragium, was a Roman execution protocol used to accelerate the death of the victim. Using a heavy iron club, the Roman executioners would
shatter the lower leg bones, specifically the tibia and fibula, of the crucified individual. Once
the legs were broken, the victim could no longer support their body weight or push upward to
breathe. Consequently, fatal traumatic asphyxiation followed within a matter of minutes.
In the historical account of the crucifixion of Jesus, the Jewish religious leaders petitioned the
Roman governor, Pontius Pilate, to have the legs of the victims broken. This request was made to expedite death so that the bodies could be removed from the crosses before the onset of the Sabbath, which was a high holy day.
Acting on Pilate's orders, the Roman soldiers executed the crurifragium procedure on the two criminals crucified alongside Jesus. However, when the executioners approached Jesus to perform the same procedure, they conducted a visual and physical assessment and
determined that He was already dead.
Because He was already deceased, the soldiers bypassed the crura fracture. To legally and
medically certify His death, one of the soldiers instead thrust a spear into His side, resulting in a sudden flow of blood and water, indicating heart failure. This exemption from the crura fracture is of monumental evidentiary value because it occurred under the strict, unyielding protocols of the Roman military machine, which had no vested interest in fulfilling Jewish
prophecies.

The non-negotiable requirement that the Passover lamb must not have any of its bones broken is established in the following two books of the Law:
Exodus 12:46: "In one house shall it be eaten; thou shalt not carry forth ought of the flesh abroad out of the house; neither shall ye break a bone thereof."
Numbers 9:12: "They shall leave none of it unto the morning, nor break any bone of it: according to all the ordinances of the passover they shall keep it."

The prophetic declaration regarding the physical integrity of the "Righteous Servant" (the Messiah) is found in the Book of Psalms:

Psalm 34:20: "He keepeth all his bones: not one of them is broken."

The New Testament confirms that these specific Old Testament statutes and prophecies were fulfilled during the crucifixion of Jesus to prove His identity:

John 19:33: "But when they came to Jesus, and saw that he was dead already, they brake not his legs."
John 19:36: "For these things were done, that the scripture should be fulfilled, A bone of him shall not be broken. 
 Conclusively, the crura fracture was a brutal but effective Roman method for inducing rapid death by
asphyxiation during crucifixion. The exemption of Jesus from this procedure was a critical
historical event that bypassed human intervention to fulfill divine prophecy. By remaining
physically unbroken, Jesus satisfied the stringent statutory requirements of the Exodus
Passover Lamb and the prophetic declarations of the Psalms. This convergence of Roman
execution protocols and ancient Jewish law provides a profound evidentiary basis for
