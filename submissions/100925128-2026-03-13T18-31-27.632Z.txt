Question 3:
The Hypostatic Union and Christ as the Perfect MediatorThe "Hypostatic Union" is the theological term used to describe the union of two distinct natures—100% Divine and 100% Human—in the one Person of Jesus Christ. The teaching explains that these two natures are not "mixed" to create a third type of being, nor are they "alternated." Instead, they exist simultaneously and perfectly in Him.

The Perfect Mediator: To be a mediator, one must relate perfectly to both parties in a dispute. 
As 100% Man: Jesus could represent humanity, feel our infirmities, and legally die in our place to pay the penalty for sin. As 100% God: His sacrifice had infinite value, and He had the power to conquer death and satisfy the divine justice of God. Conclusio Only a Being who is both God and Man can lay a hand on both and reconcile them (Job 9:33).

3. Five (5) Evidences of Jesus’ Humanity
The teaching provides evidence that Jesus possessed a real human body and human emotions:
1.  *He grew weary/tired:* As a man, He was subject to physical limitations.
    *   *Scripture:* *John 4:6* — *"Jesus therefore, being wearied with his journey, sat thus on the well."*
2.  *He experienced hunger:* He had a physical digestive system that required nourishment.
    *   *Scripture:* *Matthew 4:2* — *"And when he had fasted forty days and forty nights, he was afterward an hungred."*
3.  *He experienced thirst:* Even on the cross, His human body felt the need for water.
    John 19:28*Jesus... saith, I thirst."*He expressed human emotions (Sorrow/Weeping):* He felt the pain of loss and compassion.
    John 11:35Jesus wept."
5.  He experienced physical death: His body was capable of ceasing to function, proving He was flesh and blood.
  John 19:30* He bowed his head, and gave up the ghost."*

4. Jesus as the Visible Expression of the Invisible God
*Discussion:* The teaching uses *Hebrews 1:3* to explain that Jesus is the "express image" of God’s person. Since God is Spirit and invisible, Jesus came to "explain" or "decode" God to us. If you want to know how God thinks, acts, or feels, you look at Jesus.

*Five Examples of Jesus' Behavior Revealing the Father:*
1.  *Compassion for the Outcast:* When Jesus touched the leper (*Mark 1:41*), He revealed that the Father is not distant but touches the "untouchable."
2.  *Forgiveness for the Sinner:* By forgiving the woman caught in adultery (*John 8:11*), He showed the Father’s heart of mercy over condemnation.
3.  *Healing the Sick:* Jesus went about doing good and healing all (*Acts 10:38*), proving that it is the Father’s nature and will to heal.
4.  *Provision for the Needy:* Feeding the 5,000 (*Matthew 14*) reveals the Father as *Jehovah Jireh*, the Provider.
5.  *Love for Children:* By welcoming children (*Matthew 19:14*), He showed the Father’s accessibility and tenderness toward the humble.
