Question 2:
The Hypostatic Union is the doctrine that Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man at the same time. His divine nature and human nature exist together in one person without mixing or losing their distinct qualities.A practical example is the stones and water in a bowl; they exist together but do not mix into a new substance. Because of this union, Jesus possesses all the attributes of God and all the experiences of man. As God, He has the power and authority to save. As man, He can represent humanity and understand human weaknesses. Therefore, He stands between God and humans.This makes Him the perfect mediator between God and mankind.He represents God to humans and humans to God. Thus, the hypostatic union qualifies Christ to be the perfect mediator.

Question 6:
The crura fracture was the Roman practice of breaking the legs of a crucified person to hasten death. The soldiers broke the legs of the two criminals crucified with Jesus so they would die quickly. However, when they came to Jesus, they found that He was already dead, so His legs were not broken.
Instead, a soldier pierced His side with a spear. This fulfilled the Old Testament prophecy that none of the Messiah’s bones would be broken. The prophecy appears in Psalm 34:20. This exemption of Jesus from crura fracture served as prophetic proof that He was the promised Messiah.
